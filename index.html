I am a first year Ph.D. student working with John Laird in Soar lab at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. My research interests are Human Robotic interaction and cognitive architectures.
My research focuses on developing techniques for Rosie, an intelligent agent developed in Soar that dynamically learns new tasks, so that humans can effectively and efficiently discover and then employ its capabilities through natural interaction. It also involves Rosie developing internal mental models of what the users are expected to know about its capabilities, customized to each user. The goal of this research is to enable efficient and concise interactions between humans and Rosie in order to perform any task. My current focus is on exposing Rosie's internal knowledge thus giving the instructor an insight into its reasoning and functioning capabilities. My individual interest in this line of research comes from curiosity to learn about human intelligence.
